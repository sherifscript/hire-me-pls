VOICE REFERENCE — Jordan Park

This is a sample of Jordan's own writing, used by the cover-letter skill to
anchor tone. It is a cover letter Jordan wrote, unaided, for an earlier role.
The point is not the structure — it is the register: short sentences, plain
claims, no performed enthusiasm, willing to concede a limitation.

---

Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying because the role is squarely about onboarding, and onboarding
is the part of product work I have spent the last four years on.

At my current company I owned the new-user experience for a consumer app
with about fifteen million registered users. When I started, most new users
never came back after the first session. I spent a month just watching
session recordings before I changed anything, which felt slow at the time
and turned out to be the most useful thing I did. The fix was not clever. We
were asking for a permissions grant before the user had any reason to care.
We moved it. Activation went up and stayed up.

I am not a designer and I am not an engineer. What I am good at is finding
the one place a funnel is actually leaking and getting the team to agree to
fix that before anything else. That takes some patience and a tolerance for
being the person who says no to the other twelve ideas.

I would be glad to talk.

Best regards,
Jordan Park
