## Summary

Senior product manager, 8 years, mobile and web. Started in consumer apps,
now moving toward B2B SaaS. Strongest at activation and growth — taking a
funnel that leaks and finding where, then shipping the fix and proving it
moved. Comfortable with quantitative work but not an engineer; I live in
Amplitude and Looker, not in a code editor.

## Beta Inc — Senior Product Manager, Activation (March 2024 – Present)

Brooklyn, NY. Consumer productivity app, ~15M registered users.

Promoted into this role to own the activation and onboarding surfaces.

- Led the onboarding redesign that lifted 7-day activation from 32% to 38%
  in one quarter. The work was a full funnel teardown — we found three
  drop-off points, prioritized the second one (a permissions prompt that
  fired too early), and shipped a re-sequenced flow. TechCrunch covered it
  as a case study in product-led growth.
- Built the experimentation platform the product team now uses. Before it,
  a test took about three weeks from hypothesis to readout; now it is about
  six days. It runs 40+ concurrent tests across 7 product surfaces.
- Owned the activation roadmap end to end — quarterly OKRs, prioritization,
  and the cross-functional coordination across engineering, design,
  marketing, and analytics.

## Beta Inc — Product Manager (June 2022 – March 2024)

Brooklyn, NY. Same company, the role I was promoted out of.

- Ran the notifications and re-engagement surface. Shipped a digest
  redesign that cut notification opt-outs by 22% while holding re-engagement
  flat — a quality-over-quantity bet that worked.
- Owned the mobile-web feature parity project, coordinating a backlog
  across two engineering pods over two quarters.

## Alpha Apps — Product Manager (August 2018 – May 2022)

San Francisco, CA. Consumer photo-sharing app.

- My first PM role. Owned the creator-tools surface — the features power
  users relied on to publish and grow their audiences.
- Ran the user-research practice for the product org for two years: I set
  up the interview cadence, the usability-testing rhythm, and the
  jobs-to-be-done framework the team still uses.
- Shipped the collaborative-albums feature, which became the second-most
  used feature in the app within six months of launch.
