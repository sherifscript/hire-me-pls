# Worked example — Diagnosis for the showcase candidate

This is the diagnosis that drives the showcase CV and cover letter in `examples/showcase/`. The candidate is Jordan Park, a senior PM moving from B2C consumer apps to B2B SaaS. The target role is **Senior Product Manager, Workflow Automation** at the fictional **Northwind Operations**.

This file is illustrative — it shows what a good diagnosis looks like. Use it as a reference when writing diagnoses for real roles.

---

# Role Diagnosis — Northwind Operations | Senior Product Manager, Workflow Automation

## What is this team actually hiring to fix?

Northwind has shipped a successful workflow-automation product to mid-market operations teams and is hitting the wall every B2B SaaS hits at this stage: power users want depth they don't have, new users churn before activating, and the product roadmap is being run by the founders rather than a dedicated PM. They are hiring someone who can own the activation-and-expansion arc end-to-end — diagnose where the funnel is leaking, ship the fixes, and instrument the result — so the founders can step back to deal positioning and partnerships.

## What would a great hire deliver in their first 90 days?

A diagnosed activation funnel with the top three drop-off points named and one of them fixed (measured improvement, not just shipped change), plus a 6-month roadmap for the workflow-builder surface that the engineering team has signed off on.

## What is the actual bar?

Commercial instinct disguised as product judgment — the ability to read user behavior data and connect it to revenue outcomes, then defend the resulting prioritization in front of skeptical founders.

## Which of my credentials speaks loudest to that bar?

The Beta Inc onboarding redesign that lifted 7-day activation by 18% over a single quarter, with the public TechCrunch writeup. The numbers are the proof; the narrative is "PM who connects funnel work to revenue outcomes and ships the result fast."

## How this diagnosis drives the CV

Reading the diagnosis, the cv-tailor skill makes these choices automatically:

- **Tagline:** "Senior Product Manager | Activation · Workflow Automation · B2B SaaS" — leads with the role title from the JD; the three pillars are taken from the keywords.
- **Summary:** opens on the activation lift (lead credential), names B2B SaaS as the target market, references cross-functional shipping. Three sentences.
- **Core skills:** five rows. Row 1 = Product-led growth + experimentation. Row 2 = Roadmap ownership + stakeholder management. Row 3 = User research + funnel analysis. Row 4 = Cross-functional delivery. Row 5 = Tools (Amplitude, Mixpanel, Looker, Linear, Figma).
- **Experience slot 1 (lead):** Beta Inc — Senior PM. Lead bullet is the activation redesign with the 18% lift and the TechCrunch citation in bold. Two more bullets cover cross-functional shipping and a B2B-adjacent feature.
- **Experience slot 2:** the second Beta Inc role (continuous block — Jordan was promoted from PM to Senior PM at Beta Inc).
- **Experience slot 3:** Alpha Apps — earlier PM role at a consumer app. Bullets emphasize the analytical/user-research side that transfers to B2B.
- **Education:** unchanged degree facts, but the MSc descriptive bullet leads with the quantitative coursework relevant to product analytics.

## How this diagnosis drives the cover letter

- **Opener:** "Northwind sits at the activation cliff every workflow-automation product hits when power users want more depth and new users churn before they get there — and the 18% activation lift I shipped at Beta Inc came from working that exact problem on a consumer surface." Load-bearing on the role specifics; can't be copy-pasted.
- **Body:** one paragraph on the funnel-diagnosis work, one on the cross-functional shipping at Beta Inc, one on what Jordan would want to dig into in the first 30 days.
- **Close:** specific (offer to share the Amplitude dashboards from Beta Inc; ask one substantive question about Northwind's instrumentation stack).

This is the gap between "a tailored CV" and "a CV that obviously fits this role." The diagnosis is what closes it.
