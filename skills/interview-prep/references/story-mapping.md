# Story mapping — matching stories to question themes

The story map is section 2 of the interview prep document. It pre-decides which story the candidate tells for each likely question theme, so they are not improvising story selection under pressure.

## The format

For each of the 4–5 most likely behavioral question themes for this role:

```
Theme → Story title → one-line fit rationale → full STAR+R
```

## Picking the themes

The themes are role-specific. Derive them from the role snapshot, not from a generic list. A few examples of how the snapshot drives the themes:

- A role where the snapshot says "hold a prioritization line in front of founders" → themes include *influence without authority* and *disagreement with leadership*.
- A role where the snapshot says "first dedicated hire for this function" → themes include *building something from zero* and *operating without a playbook*.
- A role where the snapshot says "stabilize a team after a departure" → themes include *stepping into ambiguity* and *earning trust quickly*.

Pick the 4–5 themes a sharp interviewer would actually probe given this specific role. Generic themes (teamwork, conflict, failure) appear only if the role genuinely warrants them.

## Picking the story for each theme

From the story bank, select the single best story per theme. "Best" means:

1. The story genuinely demonstrates the theme (not a stretch).
2. The story's Result is strong and ideally quantified.
3. The story is recent enough to be credible at the candidate's current level.
4. No single story is used for more than one theme in the same prep document — if a story is the best fit for two themes, use it for the stronger match and find a second story for the other.

## The fit rationale

One line explaining why this story fits this theme. This is for the candidate's benefit while prepping — it reminds them what angle to lead with when they tell the story. Example:

> Theme: influence without authority
> Story: "The pricing-page experiment the data team did not want to run"
> Fit: shows the candidate moved a skeptical partner team by bringing them the experiment design rather than the conclusion — exactly the founder-prioritization dynamic in the role snapshot.

## If the story bank is thin

If the story bank does not have a good story for a theme, interview-prep does two things:

1. Notes the gap explicitly in the prep document: *"No banked story strongly fits 'operating without a playbook.' Closest is [X]. Consider running Run Story Bank Refresh, or think about whether [career moment Y] from your career file could be developed into a story."*
2. Suggests running `Run Story Bank Refresh` to pull more stories from the career file.

interview-prep never invents a story. If the bank and the career file do not contain it, the gap is reported, not filled.

## Why pre-deciding matters

Under interview pressure, the hardest cognitive task is story *selection* — scanning the whole career for the right example while also composing the answer. Pre-deciding removes that load. The candidate walks in knowing: "if they ask about conflict, I tell the pricing-page story." All they have to do in the room is tell it well.
