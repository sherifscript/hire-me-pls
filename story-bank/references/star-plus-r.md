# STAR+R — the story format

This is the shared format spec for behavioral interview stories. It is referenced by both `story-bank` (maintaining the library) and `interview-prep` (assembling a prep document).

> The canonical, fuller version of this reference lives at
> `interview-prep/references/star-plus-r.md`. This copy keeps `story-bank`
> self-contained. If the two ever diverge, the interview-prep copy wins.

## The five parts

- **S — Situation:** 2–3 sentences of context.
- **T — Task:** what the candidate was specifically responsible for.
- **A — Action:** the choices the candidate made and why. The longest part.
- **R — Result:** the outcome, quantified wherever possible.
- **Reflection:** what the candidate learned or would do differently.

## Why Reflection

STAR is standard. The added Reflection is what separates a polished answer from a rote one. Senior interviewers probe for it directly ("what would you do differently?"). It signals self-awareness and growth.

## The entry format

```
=====================================
Theme: [Stakeholder Management / Ambiguous Data / Cross-functional Delivery / ...]
Strength: [analytical rigor / influence without authority / ...]
Branch fit: [Research & Analytics / Product Management / General / ...]
-------------------------------------
S:
T:
A:
R:
Reflection:
=====================================
```

## What earns a place in the bank

A moment becomes a story when it has a real decision point, a clear personal contribution, an outcome worth stating, and something learned. Smooth projects where nothing went wrong rarely make good stories — the absence of tension means there is nothing to demonstrate.
