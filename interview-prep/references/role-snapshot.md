# Role snapshot — diagnosing the role for interview prep

The role snapshot is the interview-prep equivalent of `role-diagnosis` section 1. It is 3–4 sentences that name the real problem behind the hire. Everything else in the prep document hangs off it.

## What it is

A diagnosis of the underlying pressure driving this hire. Not a restatement of the job description. The reader (the candidate, prepping) should come away knowing what the company is actually trying to solve, so they can frame every interview answer toward that.

## How to write it

1. Read the JD top to bottom once.
2. Ask: what changed that made this role open? A person left, a function is missing, a target was missed, the company hit a growth stage it cannot staff, a market shifted.
3. Look at the team size, the reporting line, and the company's recent public signals (funding, launches, press).
4. Write 3–4 sentences naming the pressure and what a successful hire relieves.

## Why it drives the rest of the prep

- **The story map** selects stories that demonstrate the candidate has solved this kind of problem before.
- **The hard questions** are the questions a sharp interviewer asks to test whether the candidate actually understands the problem.
- **Your ask** (the questions the candidate asks back) shows the interviewer the candidate has diagnosed the role correctly.

A weak role snapshot produces generic prep. A sharp one makes the whole document feel like the candidate already gets it.

## Example

For the showcase role (Senior PM, Workflow Automation at Northwind):

> Northwind has product-market fit with mid-market operations teams but is hitting the classic B2B SaaS scaling wall: the founders still own the roadmap, power users are asking for depth the product does not have, and new users churn before they activate. This role exists so the founders can hand off the activation-and-expansion arc to a dedicated PM and step back to deal positioning and partnerships. The interview will test whether the candidate can own that arc end to end — diagnose the funnel, ship fixes, instrument results — and whether they can hold a prioritization line in front of founders who are used to making the call themselves. The real question behind every interview answer: can this person be trusted with the roadmap?

That last sentence — "the real question behind every interview answer" — is the most useful line in the snapshot. It gives the candidate a single lens for every response.
